# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We want Elegy MCP to be a respectful, practical, and welcoming open-source project.

Contributors, maintainers, and community members are expected to work together in a way that:

- values clarity over posturing
- welcomes good-faith questions and corrections
- respects different backgrounds and experience levels
- keeps technical disagreement constructive

## Expected behavior

Examples of positive behavior include:

- being respectful in discussions and reviews
- giving actionable, specific feedback
- assuming good intent unless there is clear reason not to
- accepting correction gracefully
- focusing on the project goals and current scope

## Unacceptable behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, abuse, threats, or intimidation
- discriminatory or derogatory language
- sustained disruptive behavior
- doxxing or sharing private information without permission
- bad-faith technical argument meant to belittle others
- sexualized language or attention in project spaces

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to:

- repository issues and pull requests
- discussions tied to the project
- review conversations
- other project-managed community spaces

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, contact the maintainers privately through GitHub.

When possible, include:

- what happened
- where it happened
- when it happened
- links, screenshots, or other relevant context

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they believe is appropriate to protect the project and its community, including:

- requesting a change in behavior
- removing content
- temporarily or permanently limiting participation

## Project standard

This code of conduct is intentionally concise for the bootstrap phase. It may be refined as the community grows, but the expectation of respectful collaboration applies now.
